WHY IT MATTERS HERE

Manual J for Arizona's Climate

Arizona spans Climate Zones 2–4 — from the cooling-dominated low desert to the cold, high-elevation north. In Phoenix and Tucson, summer cooling drives the design; in Flagstaff, winter heating does.

Sedona, Arizona
LOW DESERT

Phoenix & Tucson

The Valley and southern Arizona see extreme summer heat. Cooling loads dominate, and a detailed calculation keeps equipment from being oversized for the short shoulder seasons.

HIGH COUNTRY

Flagstaff & the Rim

Flagstaff and northern Arizona sit at high elevation with cold, snowy winters. Heating leads here — a statewide cooling assumption would size equipment wrong.

SOLAR GAIN

Desert Sun

Arizona's intense solar radiation adds substantial heat gain through windows and roofs. We account for orientation, glazing, and shading rather than a flat estimate.
