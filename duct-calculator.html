What is Equivalent Diameter?

The equivalent diameter is the round duct size that produces the same friction loss per unit length as the rectangular duct — not the same cross-sectional area.

Why Aspect Ratio Matters

High aspect ratios (e.g. 4:1 or greater) increase turbulence, noise, and friction. A 12×12 round is more efficient than an 18×8 even at the same equivalent diameter.

ASHRAE Equivalent Diameter Formula

De = 1.30 × (a × b)^0.625 / (a + b)^0.25
De = equivalent round duct diameter (inches)
a = width of rectangular duct (inches)
b = height of rectangular duct (inches)
Source: ASHRAE Fundamentals Handbook, Duct Design chapter
