WHY IT MATTERS HERE

Manual J for Utah's Climate

Utah spans Climate Zones 3–6 — one of the widest ranges of any western state. The Wasatch Front runs cold-winter, hot-dry-summer; St. George sits in hot desert; Park City is alpine. One statewide assumption doesn't fit.

Arches National Park, Utah
WASATCH FRONT

Salt Lake, Provo & Ogden

The Wasatch Front sees cold winters and hot, dry summers, with valley inversions in winter. Loads here are split, so the calculation has to balance both seasons carefully.

SOUTHERN DESERT

St. George

St. George sits in hot, low-elevation desert (Zone 3). Cooling dominates, and a detailed load keeps equipment from being oversized for the milder months.

MOUNTAIN

Park City & High Country

Park City and Utah's high country are alpine (Zone 6) with long, cold, snowy winters. Heating leads — a valley assumption would size equipment wrong.
