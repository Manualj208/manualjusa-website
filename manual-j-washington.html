WHY IT MATTERS HERE

Manual J for Washington's Climate

Washington spans Climate Zones 4–5, split by the Cascades. West of the mountains, the marine Puget Sound climate is mild and wet with heating-dominant loads; east of them, Spokane and Yakima run colder in winter and hotter in summer.

Seattle & Mount Rainier, Washington
MARINE WEST

Puget Sound

Seattle, Tacoma, and Bellevue sit in a mild, damp marine climate. Heating leads and cooling is modest, so loads have to reflect that balance — oversizing wastes money and hurts comfort.

CONTINENTAL EAST

Inland Washington

Spokane and the inland east see colder winters and hotter, drier summers. We calculate for those real conditions rather than a single statewide assumption.

CENTRAL VALLEYS

Yakima Valley

Central Washington's Yakima Valley runs hot and dry in summer, raising cooling demand well above the coast. We account carefully for that higher load.

Washington Manual J FAQs

Do I need a Manual J for an HVAC permit in Washington?

Most Washington jurisdictions require a Manual J load calculation for HVAC permits on new construction and equipment changeouts. Requirements vary by city and county, so we typically format every report for submission to your local building department — across the Puget Sound metro, inland east, and central valleys.
