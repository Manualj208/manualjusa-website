WHY IT MATTERS HERE

Manual J for Oregon's Climate

Oregon spans Climate Zones 4–5 — but it's really two climates. The marine-influenced Willamette Valley runs mild and wet with heating-dominant loads, while Central Oregon's high desert brings colder winters and big day-night swings.

Crater Lake, Oregon
MARINE WEST

Willamette Valley & Coast

Portland, Salem, and Eugene sit in a mild, damp marine climate. Heating leads and cooling is modest, so sizing has to reflect that balance rather than a generic split.

HIGH DESERT

Central Oregon

Bend and the high desert see colder winters, lower humidity, and large day-to-night temperature swings. We calculate loads for those real conditions, not a statewide average.

SOUTHERN VALLEYS

Rogue & Umpqua Valleys

Medford and southern Oregon run warmer and drier in summer than the coast, raising cooling demand. We account carefully for the inland valleys.
